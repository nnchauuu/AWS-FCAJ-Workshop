# Summary Report: "FCAJ Community Day"

## Event Information

- **Event name:** FCAJ Community Day
- **Date:** May 30, 2026
- **Time:** 09:00 – 12:00
- **Location:** Bitexco Financial Tower, Ho Chi Minh City
- **Role:** Participant

---

# Event Objectives

FCAJ Community Day was organized to create an opportunity for students, developers, and technology enthusiasts to connect, learn, and gain practical experience from speakers in various fields such as Cloud, Hackathon, startup, product development, software project management, and personal development.

The event aimed to achieve the following objectives:

- Share practical tools and methods to improve Cloud infrastructure deployment skills.
- Summarize real-world lessons from Hackathon competitions and the challenges of building technology products within a short period of time.
- Explore the mindset of self-management, overcoming self-doubt, procrastination, and understanding hidden issues in software project management.
- Analyze a real startup case study from identifying market problems to optimizing user experience.
- Inspire participants to continue learning, practicing technology, and developing product thinking.

---

# List of Speakers

- **The Ballers Team** - Shared practical Hackathon experience.
- **Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu** - Topic about confidence.
- **Founder of the Tu Vi Dai Viet project** - Topic about startup and business model.
- **Tran Minh Quan** - Topic about the hidden iceberg of software projects and procrastination.

# Session 1: Sharing Practical Hackathon Experience

The Ballers Team shared their experience participating in Hackathons, the pressure of building products within a short time, and practical lessons learned during the project development process.

### Main Content

- Shared the intensity of Hackathons, such as lack of sleep, continuous work, and the need to complete a product within a very limited time.
- Introduced outstanding projects such as SynthHunter, which uses AI Voice Verification, and Vortex, which supports recruitment process optimization.
- Presented the product development process from initial idea to prototype.
- Shared experience in teamwork, task division, and handling errors under time pressure.
- Emphasized the importance of solving the right real-world problem instead of only focusing on technology.

### Lessons Learned

- Hackathons are an environment that helps train practical problem-solving skills.
- Teamwork and reasonable task division help improve product development efficiency.
- Time pressure helps participants learn how to prioritize important features.
- A good product needs to start from real user needs.

---

# Session 2: The Importance of Confidence

Ms. Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu shared about confidence, self-doubt, and how to overcome psychological barriers in learning and work.

### Main Content

- Analyzed the reasons why many people lack confidence in communication and work.
- Explained the "iceberg model" of insecurity, in which the visible part is silence, while the hidden part is the fear of being judged and overthinking.
- Emphasized that confidence is built through daily actions and practice.
- Introduced the "5-second rule" to help people take action instead of hesitating.
- Encouraged participants to dare to try, dare to make mistakes, and dare to face their fears.

### Lessons Learned

- Confidence is a skill that can be developed over time.
- Action creates confidence, not the other way around.
- Insecurity often comes from fear of judgment and the habit of overthinking.
- Actively seizing opportunities is an effective way to develop oneself.

# Session 3: Startup Story from the Tu Vi Dai Viet Project

The founder of the Tu Vi Dai Viet project shared the journey of transforming a traditional service into a modern technology platform that is more user-friendly for young users.

### Main Content

- Analyzed the pain points of traditional services, such as high cost, difficulty of access, and complicated terminology.
- Digitized traditional astrology services into a platform that is easier for Gen Z users to use.
- Focused on transparent, understandable UI/UX that can be used daily.
- Applied the Freemium model with Basic, Standard, and Premium packages to expand the user base.
- Optimized user experience to turn complex concepts into a clear and structured interface.

### Lessons Learned

- A good technology product must start from real market pain points.
- UI/UX not only makes the interface more attractive but also simplifies the usage process.
- A suitable business model helps a product scale more effectively.
- Digitizing a traditional service requires a combination of user understanding, technology, and product strategy.

---

# Session 4: The Hidden Iceberg of Software Projects and Procrastination

Mr. Tran Minh Quan shared about hidden problems in software project management and the deeper causes of procrastination.

### Main Content

- Analyzed the "hidden iceberg" model in software projects.
- A feature that seems simple may generate many hidden costs because it has to be redone many times.
- The visible problems are often delayed deadlines, system errors, or bugs in the production environment.
- The hidden causes often come from unclear requirements, manual processes, and communication breakdowns between teams.
- Regarding procrastination, laziness is only a small part of the cause. Most of it comes from emotions, fear of failure, or the fear of not being good enough.

### Lessons Learned

- Procrastination is not simply laziness; it is also related to emotional management.
- Poor communication and unclear requirements can create many hidden costs in software projects.
- Requirements, processes, and responsibilities of each member need to be clarified to reduce rework.
- A project needs transparent communication and management in order to operate effectively.

# Session 5: Improving Cloud Skills with AWS Cloud Quest and Floci

This session introduced tools that support Cloud learning and practice, helping learners improve infrastructure deployment skills while reducing cost-related risks.

### Main Content

- Introduced AWS Cloud Quest, a platform for learning AWS through gamification.
- Introduced Floci, a local testing simulation tool that supports fast and lightweight testing.
- Compared simulated environments with real AWS environments.
- Emphasized the role of local testing in saving costs and improving feedback speed during software development.
- Shared experience in managing resources and costs when starting to use Cloud.

### Lessons Learned

- Effective Cloud learning should combine theory, practice, and simulation tools.
- AWS Cloud Quest makes learning AWS more visual and easier to access.
- Floci supports local testing and helps reduce the risk of unexpected costs when working with Cloud.
- Proper resource management helps limit unwanted expenses.

# Highlighted Contents of the Event

## Improving Cloud Skills and Hackathon Lessons

The event introduced Cloud learning support tools such as AWS Cloud Quest and Floci. AWS Cloud Quest helps learners approach AWS knowledge in a visual and practical way, while Floci supports fast and lightweight local testing to reduce cost-related risks.

In addition, the sharing session from The Ballers Team helped participants better understand the intensity of Hackathons. Although Hackathons require working under high pressure, lack of sleep, and continuous debugging, they still provide great value in problem-solving skills, teamwork, and real product development.

## The Power of Confidence and the Iceberg of Procrastination

The event helped participants understand that insecurity and procrastination both have hidden psychological causes. Silence, avoidance, or hesitation often comes from the fear of being judged, overthinking, fear of failure, or the feeling of not being good enough.

Through the models shared at the event, I realized that overcoming these barriers requires starting with small actions, managing emotions better, and actively facing problems instead of delaying them.

## Startup Problems and Product Strategy

The story from the Tu Vi Dai Viet project showed that a successful technology product needs to begin with identifying the correct pain points of the market. Digitizing a traditional service is not only about putting the service on a website, but also about optimizing UI/UX, business model, and daily user experience.

The Freemium model makes the product more accessible and creates opportunities to scale the service to more users instead of depending completely on manual consulting.

## Hidden Issues in Software Project Management

Another notable topic was the hidden iceberg of software projects. In many projects, the visible problems are delayed deadlines, system errors, or production bugs. However, the real causes often lie beneath the surface, such as unclear requirements, lack of communication, manual processes, and insufficient testing.

This helped me understand that modern technology is only one part of a project. For a project to succeed, it needs clear processes, good communication, and early risk control.
