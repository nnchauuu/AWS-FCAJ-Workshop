Event 2

Summary Report: “FCAJ Community Day - June 2026”

Event Information

  • Event Name: FCAJ Community Day - June 2026
  • Date: June 27, 2026
  • Format: In-person attendance
  • Location: FCAJ Community Day event venue
  • Role: Attendee

Event Objectives

FCAJ Community Day - June 2026 was organized to create an opportunity for students, developers, and people interested in technology to connect and learn practical experiences from speakers in the fields of AWS, AI, DevOps, Cloud Architecture, and technology product development.

The event aimed to achieve the following goals:

  • Introduce current trends in applying AI to real-world products.
  • Share experience in building AI Voice Agents capable of natural conversations.
  • Explore the challenges of deploying AI systems into production environments.
  • Study how tools, data, and enterprise systems can be connected through MCP.
  • Provide practical perspectives on AWS, Cloud Architecture, DevOps, and system operations.
  • Inspire attendees to keep learning, practicing technology, and developing product thinking.

Session 1: Voice Agents - Building Human-Like AI Conversations at Scale

This session focused on building AI Voice Agents that can communicate naturally with users, especially in Vietnamese-language contexts and systems that require real-time responses.

Key Topics

  • Overview of AI Voice Agents and their role in modern technology products.
  • Analysis of the conversation processing flow, including Speech-to-Text, Large Language Models, and Text-to-Speech.
  • Challenges in building natural Vietnamese conversations.
  • The importance of latency when the system needs to respond in real time.
  • Experience handling interruptions, context, emotion, and tone in conversations.
  • How Voice Agents can connect with tools to perform business actions.

Key Takeaways

  • Building an AI Voice Agent is not only about making a model speak, but also about ensuring natural, contextual, and fast conversations.
  • Latency is a critical factor that directly affects user experience.
  • Production AI systems need to balance response quality, infrastructure cost, and scalability.
  • Vietnamese real-time conversations introduce challenges in tone, context, and response speed.

Session 2: Building Secure Private MCP Connection

This session introduced MCP and how to build private, secure connections between AI Agents and internal tools or systems.

Key Topics

  • Introduction to MCP and its role in the AI Agent ecosystem.
  • How AI Agents can connect to external tools to perform real-world tasks.
  • Security requirements when connecting AI with enterprise data and systems.
  • Experience building private MCP connections to reduce the risk of data leakage.
  • How MCP can be combined with AWS services in system architecture.
  • The role of authorization, access control, and data protection when deploying AI Agents.

Key Takeaways

  • MCP allows AI Agents to do more than answer questions; they can interact with tools and perform actions.
  • Security and authorization are essential when connecting AI to real systems.
  • Private connections help reduce risks when working with internal data.
  • AI Agents must be designed with proper controls to avoid incorrect data access or unintended actions.

Session 3: AWS, AI, DevOps, and Cloud Architecture

This session provided an overview of how AWS, AI, and DevOps can be combined to build modern systems that are scalable and easier to operate.

Key Topics

  • The role of AWS in deploying modern AI and backend systems.
  • How Cloud Architecture supports scaling when user traffic grows.
  • The importance of DevOps in automating deployment and operations.
  • Important considerations when bringing AI models into production.
  • Experience balancing performance, cost, and system stability.
  • How to monitor, test, and improve systems after deployment.

Key Takeaways

  • AWS provides many services for deployment, scaling, and system monitoring.
  • DevOps helps software development and deployment become faster and more stable.
  • Cloud Architecture should be designed based on real needs instead of adding unnecessary complexity.
  • AI deployment requires attention to latency, security, cost, and scalability.

Technologies and Concepts Introduced

Throughout the event, many modern technologies and concepts were introduced, including:

  • AWS Cloud
  • AI Voice Agent
  • Speech-to-Text
  • Large Language Model
  • Text-to-Speech
  • MCP
  • Amazon Q
  • DevOps
  • Cloud Architecture
  • Production AI System

These technologies support the development of modern AI systems that can interact with users, connect with business tools, and operate on Cloud platforms.

Overall Learning Outcomes

After participating in the event, I gained a clearer understanding of how real-world AI systems are built, especially systems that communicate through voice and integrate with enterprise tools.

Some important lessons include:

  • AI Voice Agents need to be designed for natural, contextual, and fast conversations.
  • Production AI systems must consider latency, cost, scalability, and security.
  • MCP helps AI Agents connect with external tools to perform real actions.
  • AWS and DevOps play important roles in deploying, monitoring, and operating systems.
  • When building AI products, it is necessary to balance technical demos with real deployment feasibility.
  • A modern system needs a clear architecture, scalability, and risk control.

Applying the Knowledge

The knowledge gained from the event can be applied to my learning and project work in the following ways:

  • Learn more about building chatbots or AI-integrated voice assistants.
  • Apply scalable system design thinking when deploying on AWS.
  • Pay more attention to security when systems need to access internal data or tools.
  • Study MCP to understand how AI Agents connect with external systems.
  • Apply DevOps practices to automate build, test, and deployment workflows.
  • Consider practicality, operability, and deployment cost when working on projects.
  • Design systems in a simple way first, then expand gradually based on real needs.

Event Experience

Attending FCAJ Community Day - June 2026 in person was a very valuable experience because I had the opportunity to listen to practical sharing from speakers, observe the event atmosphere, and connect with the community learning AWS, AI, and Cloud.

I was especially impressed by the session about AI Voice Agents because the topic did not stop at creating a demo that can talk. It also analyzed real-world issues such as latency, interruption handling, conversation context, and operating cost. These factors are very important when bringing an AI system into real use.

The MCP session also helped me understand that future AI Agents will not only respond with text, but can also connect with tools, data, and systems to perform specific tasks. This opens many new directions for software development, workflow automation, and user support.

Participating in person also helped me clearly feel the learning spirit of the FCAJ community. The event atmosphere gave me more motivation to continue exploring AWS, AI, DevOps, and Cloud Architecture for my studies, projects, and future career direction.

Overall, the event helped me expand my knowledge of AWS, AI, DevOps, and Cloud Architecture, while providing a more practical experience than only watching recorded videos.

Lessons Learned

Through the event, I learned several important lessons:

  • A good AI product needs to solve real problems, not just remain a demo.
  • When deploying AI into production, latency, cost, security, and scalability must be considered.
  • Voice Agents need to handle context, emotion, and real-time responses well.
  • MCP is an important approach that helps AI Agents connect with tools and external systems.
  • AWS and DevOps are important foundations for stable and scalable system operations.
  • Learning new technology should combine theory, hands-on practice, and observation of real case studies.
  • Modern technology needs to go together with product thinking, clear processes, and risk management.

Conclusion

FCAJ Community Day - June 2026 was an event that provided many useful insights into AWS, AI, DevOps, and Cloud Architecture. The sessions helped me better understand how to build real-world AI systems, especially AI Voice Agents and MCP in the context of modern product deployment.

Through the event, I realized that developing a successful technology product requires a combination of technical knowledge, architectural thinking, security, operability, and user experience. These are important lessons that can be applied to my studies, projects, and career orientation in Information Technology.
